Understanding Safe Disposal
⚠️
Why Antibiotics Need Special Handling

Antibiotics in water and soil accelerate antimicrobial resistance, which kills 1.27 million people per year globally. Never flush or throw antibiotics in regular waste.

💧
Risks of Improper Disposal

Medicines flushed down drains contaminate water supplies. Bacteria exposed to low antibiotic levels develop resistance, making infections harder to treat.

🏥
Why Pharmacy Return Programs Matter

Pharmacy take-back programs ensure medicines are disposed through regulated incineration. This prevents environmental contamination and protects community health.

Antibiotic Disposal Guidelines
  1. Do not flush antibiotics down the toilet or sink.
  2. Do not throw antibiotics in household dustbins.
  3. Keep in original packaging until return.
  4. Return to a verified pharmacy take-back point.
  5. If expired, inform the pharmacist during return.
💡
Unfinished Antibiotic Course?

Incomplete antibiotic courses can contribute to antimicrobial resistance. If you have leftover antibiotics, do not save them for later use. Return them safely to a pharmacy.

🏥

For antibiotics, we strongly recommend returning to a pharmacy take-back program. This ensures proper regulated disposal and prevents environmental contamination.

    Home Disposal Guide

    ⚠️ Never flush medicines down the toilet or pour them down drains. Do not throw antibiotics in household dustbins.
    1. 1 Remove medicines from original packaging.
    2. 2 Mix with coffee grounds, dirt, or cat litter.
    3. 3 Seal in a non-transparent, closed bag.
    4. 4 Dispose in your household waste bin (NOT recycling).
